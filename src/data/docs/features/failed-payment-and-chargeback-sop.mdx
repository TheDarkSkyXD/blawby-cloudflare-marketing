# Failed Payment & Chargeback SOP

Every law firm eventually hits payment declines or disputes. The difference between a small issue and a messy one is whether your team follows a consistent process.

This SOP gives you a practical, repeatable response flow.

## Why This SOP Matters

Without a standard response process, teams often:

- Contact clients inconsistently
- Miss dispute deadlines
- Lose track of evidence
- Escalate stress across billing and legal staff

With a clear SOP, you protect revenue and preserve client relationships.

## Ownership Rules (Set Once)

Assign:

- Primary billing owner
- Backup owner
- Escalation contact (attorney or operations lead)

Define internal SLA:

- Initial review: same day
- First client contact: within one business day
- Final escalation decision: within three business days

## Failed Payment Process

## Step 1: Confirm Failure Type

Identify whether the issue is:

- Card decline
- ACH return
- Expired card/payment method
- Temporary processing issue

Why this matters: resolution path differs by failure type.

## Step 2: Update Internal Status

Immediately update payment status in your dashboard and internal tracking system:

- `Failed - retry pending`
- `Failed - client action required`
- `Escalated`

Why this matters: status visibility prevents duplicate outreach.

## Step 3: Send First Client Message

Use clear, neutral language:

“We were unable to process your payment today. Please update your payment method using this secure link so we can keep your matter moving.”

Include:

- Exact balance due
- Payment link
- Clear due date for update

Why this matters: fast, specific communication resolves most failures without conflict.

## Step 4: Retry and Escalate

Recommended cadence:

1. First contact (day 0-1)
2. Friendly reminder (day 2-3)
3. Final notice + escalation review (day 5-7)

Escalate to attorney/operations lead if:

- No response after final notice
- Repeated failures across billing cycle
- Matter deadlines are impacted

## Chargeback Process

## Step 1: Log and Acknowledge Immediately

When a dispute appears:

- Record date, amount, client, and invoice/matter
- Assign owner
- Confirm response deadline

Why this matters: missed deadlines usually mean automatic loss.

## Step 2: Collect Supporting Records

Gather:

- Invoice or payment request details
- Engagement agreement/payment terms
- Communication timeline
- Proof of delivered work or scheduled consultation

Why this matters: organized evidence improves your chance of successful resolution.

## Step 3: Submit Response on Time

Submit all required documents before the dispute deadline and log submission details internally.

Why this matters: late or incomplete responses weaken your position.

## Step 4: Close and Learn

After resolution:

- Update outcome (`won`, `lost`, `client resolved`)
- Note root cause
- Adjust client communication or billing terms if needed

Why this matters: each dispute should improve future process quality.
